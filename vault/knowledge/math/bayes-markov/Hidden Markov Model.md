*Also known as HMM, it is a type of [[Markov Model|Markov model]] for sequential data where the system transitions between unobservable (hidden) states, each generating a sequence of observable outputs.*

A [[Markov Chain]] with hidden states that produce observable emissions. Widely used in [[Natural Language Processing|NLP]], speech recognition, and bioinformatics.
## Components
- **Hidden States:** A finite set of internal states $S = \{s_1, s_2, \dots, s_N\}$ the system can be in, which you can't directly see (e.g., a person's mood, weather conditions).
- **Emissions/Observations:** A set of observable outputs $O = \{o_1, o_2, \dots, o_M\}$ generated by the hidden states (e.g., words spoken, weather measurements).
- **Transition Probabilities:** The probabilities of moving from one hidden state to another, represented by a matrix $A$ where $A_{ij} = P(s_j | s_i)$ (e.g., probability of rain tomorrow given it's sunny today).
- **Emission Probabilities:** The probabilities of observing a particular output from a hidden state, represented by a matrix $B$ where $B_{jk} = P(o_k | s_j)$ (e.g., probability of hearing "happy" when in a happy mood).
## Application in [[Knowledge Representation#Part-of-Speech (PoS) Tagging|PoS Tagging]]
The most probable sequence is given by: $$ (t_1, \dots , t_{n}) = \arg\max_{t_1, \dots , t_{n}} P(t_1, \dots , t_{n} | w_1, \dots , w_{n}) $$Applying Bayes' theorem: $$ = \arg\max_{t_1, \dots , t_{n}} P(w_1, \dots , w_{n} | t_1, \dots , t_{n}) P(t_1, \dots , t_{n}) $$Using the [[Markov Chain#Assumptions|first-order Markov assumption]] and the output independence assumption (the probability of a word occurring is independent from neighboring words and tags): $$ \approx \arg\max_{t_1, \dots , t_{n}} \prod_{i=1}^{n} P(w_i | t_i) P(t_i | t_{i-1}) $$Where $P(w_i | t_i)$ is the emission probability (*if we were to generate a word for $t_i$, which is the probability of selecting the word $w_i$?* - can be expanded using [[Bayes’ Theorem|Bayes' theorem]]) and $P(t_i | t_{i-1})$ is the transition probability.
