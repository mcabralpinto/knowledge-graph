*A well-known and well-understood framework for quantifying [[Uncertainty|uncertainty]].*
## Interpretations
There are several interpretations of probability, the two most common being:
- **Frequentist Probability:** defines probability as the long-run relative frequency of an event occurring in repeated independent trials. For example, the probability of getting heads when flipping a fair coin is 0.5 because, over many flips, heads will appear approximately half the time.
- **Subjective Probability:** defines probability as a measure of an individual's degree of belief in the occurrence of an event, based on their knowledge and information. For example, if a doctor believes there is a 70% chance that a patient has a certain disease based on symptoms and test results, this reflects their subjective probability.
## Concepts
- **Random Variable:** a variable that can take on different values based on the outcome of a random event.
- **Atomic Event:** a single outcome of a random experiment, which may comprise several random variables.
- **Joint Distribution:** a probability distribution that gives the probability of each possible combination of values for a set of random variables.
- **Prior Probability:** the initial probability assigned to an event before any new evidence is taken into account.
- **Posterior/Conditional Probability:** the probability of an event occurring given that another event has occurred - $P(A|B)$.
