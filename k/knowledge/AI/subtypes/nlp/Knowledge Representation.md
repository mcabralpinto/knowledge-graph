*Field of [[Artificial Intelligence|AI]] dedicated to (formally) representing information about the world, so that it can be utilized by a computer to solve tasks it wouldn't be able to otherwise. Powered by [[Knowledge Extraction]].*

**Reasoning:** Humans use knowledge to make decisions, reason, and communicate. Machines can do the same with structured representations of said knowledge.
## Representation Methods
- **Raw Text:** The simplest form of knowledge representation, where information is stored as unprocessed text. While easy to obtain, it lacks structure and is difficult for machines to interpret and reason about. Is often interpreted as a **Bag-of-Words (BoW)**, ignoring word order and grammar.
- **Propositional Logic:** A formal system in which statements (propositions) can be either true or false. It uses logical connectives (AND, OR, NOT, IMPLIES) to build complex expressions and allows for reasoning about the truth values of these expressions.
- **First-Order Logic:** An extension of propositional logic that includes quantifiers (like "for all" and "there exists") and predicates, allowing for more expressive representations of knowledge about objects and their relationships.
- **[[Ontologies & Vocabularies]]:** Structured frameworks that define the concepts, properties, and relationships within a specific domain, providing a shared vocabulary for representing knowledge.
- **Semantic Networks:** Graph structures where nodes represent concepts or entities, and edges represent relationships between them. They are used to model knowledge in a way that is easy to visualize and reason about (e.g. [[Knowledge Graph]]).
- **Frames:** Data structures that represent stereotypical situations or objects, consisting of slots (attributes) and fillers (values). Frames can also include default values and inheritance mechanisms.
- **[[Embedding#Word Embeddings|Word Embeddings]]:** Dense vector representations of words that capture semantic relationships and contextual information, enabling machines to understand and process natural language more effectively at the cost of human interpretability.
