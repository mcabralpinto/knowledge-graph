*A set of components that interact with each other.*
## Concepts
- **[[Model]]:** Simplified representation of reality. We build them to understand (explain, predict, simulate, reason about, control) systems.
- **Simple System:** predictable, easy to understand, clear rules (*e.g., a recipe*).
- **Complicated System:** has many parts, require expertise, difficult but still predictable (*e.g., a plane*). 
- **Complex System:** involves interactions that create unpredictable behavior, adapt and change over time, cannot be fully understood by analyzing parts in isolation (*e.g., a society*). Can be defined as a system that exhibits non-trivial [[System#Dynamics|emergent / self-organizing]] behaviors.
- **Complex Adaptive System:** a complex system that can adapt and evolve in response to changes in its environment or internal structure (*e.g., an ecosystem*).
## Types
- **Linear vs. Non-linear:** A system is linear if outputs are proportional to inputs, effects add up cleanly, the whole equals the sum of the parts. $$F(\alpha x_1 + \beta x_2) = \alpha F(x_1) + \beta F(x_2) \quad \text{(Superposition Principle)}$$A non-linear system does not satisfy this principle, meaning that small changes in input can lead to disproportionately large changes in output, and the whole is not simply the sum of its parts.
- **Deterministic vs. Stochastic:** A system is deterministic if its future behavior is fully determined by its current state and rules, with no randomness involved. In contrast, a stochastic system incorporates random variables and probabilistic outcomes, making its future behavior uncertain even if the current state is known.
- **Open vs. Closed vs. Isolated:** An open system exchanges matter and energy with its surroundings, a closed system exchanges only energy, and an isolated system does not exchange either matter or energy with its surroundings.
## Properties
- **Robust:** A system is robust if it can maintain its behavior and functionality in the face of perturbations or changes in its environment or internal structure.
- **Evolvable:** A system is evolvable if it can adapt and change over time in response to environmental changes or internal modifications.
- **Stable:** A system is stable if it returns to a steady state after being disturbed.
## Dynamics
System dynamics is the study of how systems change over time, including the feedback loops, delays, and interactions that influence system behavior. It focuses on understanding the internal structure and behavior of systems rather than just their external appearance.
- **Change:** The behavior of a system over time is determined by its internal structure and the interactions between its components. Changes in system behavior are driven by feedback loops, delays, and the accumulation of stocks and flows within the system.
- **Emergence:** The phenomenon where complex behaviors or properties arise from the interactions of simpler components in a system, which cannot be predicted or explained by examining the individual components alone.
- **Self-organization:** The process by which a system spontaneously organizes itself without external control or central authority, often through local interactions among components.
