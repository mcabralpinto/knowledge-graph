*Fundamental concept in probability that describes how to update the likelihood of a hypothesis as more evidence or information becomes available.*

![](data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAP///wAAACH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAICRAEAOw==)
Uses the formula: $P(A | B) = \frac{P(B | A) \times P(A)}{P(B)}$.

It calculates the conditional probability of event A given that event B has occurred ($P(A | B)$) by combining prior knowledge ($P(A)$) with new data, finding the probability of the evidence given the hypothesis ($P(B | A)$), and normalizing by the overall probability of the evidence ($P(B)$).
